# The Solar System

The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it. It formed approximately 4.6 billion years ago from the gravitational collapse of a giant interstellar molecular cloud.

## The Sun

The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star (G2V) that contains 99.86% of the system's known mass. It is composed primarily of hydrogen (73%) and helium (25%), with trace amounts of heavier elements. The Sun's core temperature is approximately 15 million degrees Celsius, where nuclear fusion converts hydrogen into helium, releasing enormous amounts of energy.

## Inner Planets

### Mercury
Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System and the closest to the Sun. Its orbital period around the Sun is 87.97 Earth days, the shortest of all planets. Mercury has no atmosphere to retain heat, resulting in surface temperatures ranging from -173°C at night to 427°C during the day.

### Venus
Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is similar in structure and size to Earth, earning it the nickname "Earth's twin." However, its thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide creates a runaway greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet with surface temperatures around 462°C.

### Earth
Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. Its surface is 71% water, and it has a nitrogen-oxygen atmosphere. Earth's axial tilt of 23.5° creates seasonal variations.

### Mars
Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun. Known as the "Red Planet" due to iron oxide (rust) on its surface, Mars has the largest volcano (Olympus Mons, 21.9 km high) and the longest canyon (Valles Marineris, 4000 km long) in the Solar System.

## The Asteroid Belt

Between Mars and Jupiter lies the asteroid belt, a region populated by millions of rocky bodies. The largest object in the belt is the dwarf planet Ceres, with a diameter of about 940 km.

## Outer Planets

### Jupiter
Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System, with a mass more than twice that of all other planets combined. It is primarily composed of hydrogen and helium. Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a storm that has been raging for at least 350 years.

### Saturn
Saturn is the second-largest planet and is famous for its prominent ring system. These rings are made mostly of ice particles, rocky debris, and dust. Saturn has at least 146 known moons, with Titan being the largest.

### Uranus
Uranus is unique among the planets because it rotates on its side, with an axial tilt of 97.77°. It was the first planet discovered using a telescope, by William Herschel in 1781.

### Neptune
Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun. It has the strongest winds of any planet, reaching speeds of 2,100 km/h. Neptune's moon Triton is one of the coldest objects in the Solar System.

## Dwarf Planets

Beyond Neptune lies the Kuiper Belt, home to several dwarf planets including Pluto, Eris, Makemake, and Haumea. Pluto was reclassified from planet to dwarf planet in 2006.

## Comets

Comets are icy bodies that develop visible atmospheres (comas) and tails when they approach the Sun. The Oort Cloud, a theoretical spherical shell of icy objects, is believed to be the source of long-period comets.
